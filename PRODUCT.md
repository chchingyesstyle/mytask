# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo IT professional managing their own workload: support tickets, internal projects, vendor meetings. Uses this daily, often under time pressure. The tool is a personal command center, not a collaboration surface. Context is usually at a desk, full screen, during active work hours.

## Product Purpose

Personal AI task manager: capture, organize, and act on tasks across projects. The AI layer (chat, daily briefing, action buttons) is a genuine workflow accelerant, not a gimmick. Success is measured by how fast a user gets from "I need to track this" to "this is done."

## Brand Personality

Focused and serious. Calm, no-nonsense. Gets out of the way and lets the user work. Like a well-made physical tool: no decorative elements, no encouragement theater. Confidence through restraint.

## Anti-references

- **Jira / enterprise-dense**: Everything visible at once, nested menus, constant visual noise. This tool must feel lighter and faster.
- **SaaS-generic dark**: Dark navy/purple panels + glowing blue accent + gradient cards. The "modern dashboard" cliché. Currently the biggest risk — the existing design skews here.

## Design Principles

1. **Information at a glance**: The user is often busy. Hierarchy must make urgency legible in under a second.
2. **Speed over ceremony**: No hover states that reveal primary actions. No confirmation dialogs for low-stakes operations. Trust the user.
3. **Restraint signals confidence**: White space is not wasted space. Decorative elements erode trust in a serious tool.
4. **Dark serves the work**: Dark mode is the default because it reduces ambient light fatigue during long sessions — not because it looks cool.
5. **Earns its complexity**: Every view (board, calendar, timeline, table) earns its existence by being genuinely better for a specific job. Nothing decorative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Single user, but keyboard navigation matters (power user habits). No known color-blindness accommodation requirements, but avoid relying on red/green alone for status.
